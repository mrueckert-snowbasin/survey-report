Week in Review — Season Finale

Our final weekly NPS came in at 58.14, down from last week's 62.64 (-4.50) on 344 responses. Snow Quality crashed to -44.51, the worst weekly reading of the entire season, as warm temperatures and rapidly deteriorating conditions marked the final days of operations. Mountain NPS dropped to 27.19 (-10.91), and F&B pulled back to 23.48 (-7.10). The season average finished at 63.10 on 4,116 total responses.

The encouraging final-week story: Likely to Return recovered to 60.29, up from last week's 57.77 (+2.52), reversing the season-low reading from two weeks ago (47.02). This suggests that despite a historically challenging snow year, guests' long-term relationship with Snowbasin remains intact. Lessons surged to 64.29 (+24.82), and Safety ticked up to 58.73 (+0.52). Parking held steady at 58.70 (+0.92).

The final season comparison: 63.10 vs. last year's 65.90, a gap of just 2.80 points. In a season defined by historically low snowfall, record-warm temperatures, and limited terrain, finishing within 3 points of a good-snow season is a testament to the operational improvements the team has delivered across Service, Parking, Safety, and F&B.

The Season in One Stat: Snow Quality NPS finished at -31.69 this year vs. +19.08 last year — a 50.77-point gap. Yet the overall NPS gap was only 2.80 points. The team absorbed a 50-point weather handicap and turned it into a 3-point season. That's the story of 2025-26.

Comment Analysis — 268 Total Comments

This week's comments (92 dissatisfaction responses + 176 additional comments) deliver a fitting season finale: overwhelming gratitude for the team's effort, paired with honest feedback about a historically challenging year. Here are the key themes.

Themes of Praise

  1. Staff Excellence — The #1 theme every single week. "Employees have been very friendly and welcoming." "Your employee's attitudes and willingness to help was one of the strongest I've ever seen." Staff is consistently cited as what sets Snowbasin apart. This week, Tess and Rick in Rentals, Bob in Lessons, and Holly at Cinnabar received specific praise.
  2. Resilience Despite Snow — "Given the limited amount of snow, the crew did an incredible job getting runs open and groomed." "I have been so impressed with how Snowbasin has managed the snow and coverage." "You done the best you could with the little snow. Thankyou ski patrol." Guests overwhelmingly separate the weather from the operation.
  3. Facilities & Lodges — "The bathrooms are amazing!" "Beautiful resort. The lodges are top notch." "Snowbasin is honestly one of the best resorts I have ever visited in the United States." An east coast visitor called the lodges "the most gorgeous of anywhere I have been." Bathrooms continue earning specific praise.
  4. Free Parking — "Love the free parking, please continue to offer that!" "Please do not change the parking." "I only ski Snowbasin. It is so easy to access." Guests explicitly connect parking to their loyalty. One guest pleads: "if you implement reserved or paid parking that'd about be the only thing that would cause me to ski elsewhere."
  5. Grooming & Snowmaking — "Given the limited snow, the crew that worked on the slopes and making snow did an incredible job." The grooming team earned praise every week this season — a major achievement given the conditions they had to work with.
  6. Ski Patrol — "Ski patrol is amazing! I was rescued and sledded off the mountain by kind, considerate and professional ski patrollers who responded within minutes." Another guest notes patrol did "a great job assisting" after a collision injury. A touching comment from the rescued guest: "I'm already getting ready for next season."
  7. Park Crew & Uphill Access — "The park crew is doing a great job with the limited snow." Uphill skiing gets specific praise: "Keep the uphill going. I love doing a tour to the top to see the sunrise." Learn & Earn kids praised the beginner terrain park under Littlecat.

Themes of Concern

  1. Snow Conditions / Limited Terrain — Dominant theme. The season's story. "Worst ski season of my life." "Too low of elevation, too warm, gets rain." Climate change concerns becoming more explicit. Not actionable for weather, but the snowmaking infrastructure investment question is: "essential that Snowbasin spend the money to upgrade their snow making infrastructure."
  2. Season Pass Renewal & Value — Continued from prior weeks. Multiple explicit requests for a returning passholder discount. A senior pass holder since age 19 questions affordability. A local asks why Snowbasin doesn't match Brighton's renewal incentives. A family with $2,400 in passes notes they only skied 6 times. Timely with 26-27 passes on sale.
  3. Out-of-Control Skiers / Safety — A critical care physician wrote an extraordinary letter about collision culture, citing two deaths he's aware of and describing systemic issues. A 79-year-old was hit. Three members of a six-person group had near-misses in two runs. Sweet Revenge intersection specifically cited as dangerous. Green jacket enforcement remains polarized.
  4. F&B Pricing & Declining Quality — "$22 for a hamburger and it wasn't very good." "$9 for a pizza slice, paper thin." "Food quality and portions gone down over the years as pricing increases." Several guests now pack their own food. One guest notes Deer Valley is cheaper. A 10+ year guest says pizza "is not the same" and quality has declined.
  5. Private Events Displacing Guests — A wedding closed Needles and Earl's Lodge interior during a storm, forcing guests outside. Cinnabar closed early for the same event. This is the second week with private event frustration. Guests feel displaced without advance notice. "Would have been nice to get out of the weather."
  6. JP Lift & Early Closure — "Very disappointed in having the John Paul lift closed during the week the last two weeks." "Your GM is not a skier. He's closed lifts to save money. There is decent coverage on runs where lifts are closed." The perception of premature operational wind-down frustrated loyal pass holders.
  7. Trail Management — "If you're keeping trails open, obstacles should be marked." "Many areas unsafe to ski — unmarked." "Trails should have been closed due to lack of snow." Alpine ski team closing two runs when only 30% of mountain was open drew sharp criticism.
  8. Green Coat Polarization — Still split. "Make corridors for slow zones, not entire runs." vs. "I appreciate the speed check on beginner trails." "Holier-than-thou mountain safety host" vs. "I wish there were more green jackets." The enforcement model works for many but creates friction for experienced skiers who feel unfairly targeted.

Season Wrap-Up & Offseason Priorities

STRATEGIC — Snowmaking Infrastructure: Multiple guests this week and throughout the season have raised snowmaking capacity as the critical investment. With climate trends suggesting more variable winters, the ability to make snow in multiple locations simultaneously would directly address the resort's biggest vulnerability: its lower base elevation relative to Cottonwood competitors. This was the single most-cited structural concern this season.
STRATEGIC — Season Pass Loyalty Program: The renewal discount/loyalty theme appeared every week after the 26-27 sale launched. Guests are comparing to Brighton, Solitude, and other resorts offering renewal incentives. The comments make clear: guests love the resort and the staff, but they want to feel valued as returning customers. A loyalty acknowledgment, even a personal communication, would resonate.
OPERATIONAL — Green Coat Enforcement Model: The polarization is real: some guests love it, others say it's driving them away. Consider a "corridor" approach (slow zones on portions of runs, not entire runs) and ensure enforcement is warm/educational rather than punitive. The critical care physician's letter offers a valuable outside perspective worth discussing.
OPERATIONAL — F&B Quality & Consistency: Food quality concerns appeared more frequently toward the end of the season. The 10+ year guest who says pizza "is not the same" represents a real trend. Menu availability inconsistency (items listed but unavailable) erodes trust. Review supply chain, prep protocols, and staffing for next season's opening.
OPERATIONAL — Private Event Communication: Two instances of events displacing guests without notice. Establish a guest-facing notification protocol: email pass holders, post signage at lot entrances, and ensure alternative seating/shelter is available when lodges are reserved.
REVIEW — Porcupine Lift: Safety concerns about Porcupine appeared in multiple weeks. Loading procedures, roller stop clearance for helmeted riders, and the steep unload all warrant a summer review. Multiple guests also requested Porcupine as the next lift replacement priority.
REVIEW — Beginner/Intermediate Terrain Flow: The jump from Little Cat to Wildcat is described as too steep. Ea-sa-long and Penny Lane too narrow for mixed traffic. Sweet Revenge intersection cited by multiple guests as a danger zone. Trail routing and signage improvements could help next season.

Looking Ahead to 2026-27

The 2025-26 season closes with a clear verdict: the team delivered an exceptional operation in the face of historically poor conditions. A 63.10 NPS in a season where Snow Quality averaged -31.69 is remarkable. Six of ten controllable categories finished ahead of last year's better-snow season. Likely to Return held at 58.74, essentially matching last year's 59.09.

The season produced 4,116 survey responses vs. last year's 6,044 — a 32% decline reflecting lower visitation from snow conditions. The response mix shifted significantly toward Snowbasin pass holders (Premier + Value = 44% of respondents vs. ~36% last year), meaning local, frequent visitors had an outsized voice in this season's data.

The comments paint the path forward with unusual clarity. Guests love this resort. They love the staff. They love the facilities. What they want is investment in snow reliability (snowmaking), recognition for their loyalty (renewal incentives), consistent F&B quality, and continued safety enforcement that protects without alienating. These are the levers that will determine whether the goodwill from this season converts into next year's pass sales and visits.

Thank you to every team member who made the best of a tough year. The guests noticed, and they said so — 4,116 times.
