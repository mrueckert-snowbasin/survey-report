Week in Review

This was a milestone week for survey engagement: 546 responses, the highest single-week total this season, pushing our season total to 3,629. The surge coincided with the 2026-27 season pass launch, and the respondent mix tells the story: Snowbasin pass holders (Premier + Value) jumped from 33% to 53% of weekly respondents, many clearly weighing their renewal decision while conditions and value remain top of mind.

Our weekly NPS came in at 62.64, essentially flat from last week's 63.14 (-0.50). Snow Quality improved from -37.12 to -28.47 (+8.65) with some weekend snowfall, and Mountain NPS edged up to 38.10 (+2.36). The bright spot: Likely to Return bounced back sharply to 57.77 from last week's season-low 47.02 (+10.75), now sitting above last year's comparable (55.58). That worst-case scenario did not sustain.

The biggest concern this week is an F&B regression across all outlets: overall F&B NPS dropped from 37.10 to 30.58 (-6.52), with John Paul Lodge falling the hardest (50.85 to 24.72). Higher weekend volume drove seating and food speed pressure. On the positive side, Tune Shop surged to 68.63 (+18.63), Rentals posted a strong 63.46 (+7.05), and Retail improved to 59.26 (+3.47).

Season Pass Renewal Is This Week's Defining Theme. At least 8 separate comments specifically request a renewal discount, loyalty program, or retention incentive. A 12-year pass holder says they may skip next year. A 10-year holder is looking elsewhere. Several cite competitors offering renewal discounts Snowbasin does not. With the 26-27 pass sale now live, this feedback has immediate strategic relevance.
Season Context: The YoY gap widened to -1.21 (63.76 vs. 64.97), though this reflects last year's strong March rather than any deterioration on our side. Last year's comparable weekly NPS was 70.57 following productive snowfall we simply haven't had. Controllable metrics remain well ahead: Service (+3.18 YoY), Parking (+9.80), Safety (+3.93), F&B (+5.24), Rentals (+4.13).

Week-over-Week Shifts: Value Pass dropped sharply from 77.78 to 57.27 (-20.51). Premier Pass fell from 67.14 to 59.66 (-7.48). Both local pass types are registering the lowest NPS among segments. Meanwhile, Mountain Collective surged from 41.67 to 73.68 (+32.01, on 19 responses) and Basin Flex Card posted 84.21 (19 responses).

Season Picture: Basin Flex Card leads the season at 69.57, followed by Ikon at 69.52. Premier Pass remains the lowest Snowbasin pass type at 60.61 — down from 60.73 last week. The pass sale timing makes this segment the most important to understand. Their frustration is not primarily about the experience (Service and lodge praise remain universal); it's about the value equation in a low-snow year and the absence of loyalty recognition.

Respondent Mix Shift: Premier Pass holders surged from 70 to 177 respondents this week (up 153%), and Value Pass from 37 to 112 (up 203%). This composition change alone partially explains the overall NPS softening — it's not that the experience degraded, it's that the people most affected by the snow year are the ones responding in higher numbers as they weigh renewal.

Positives

Likely to Return Bounced Back to 57.77 — Up +10.75 from last week's season-low 47.02. Now above last year's comparable 55.58 (+2.19). This is the most important positive signal of the week, indicating last week's reading was an outlier rather than a new baseline.
Snow Quality Improved to -28.47 — Up +8.65 from -37.12. Weekend snow provided meaningful relief. Still far from positive, but trending in the right direction. Multiple comments note conditions were better later in the period.
Tune Shop Surged to 68.63 — Up +18.63 from 50.00, posting the highest single-outlet NPS reading of the week. The season average of 65.12 is +1.90 above last year's 63.22. One comment specifically praised fast and excellent wax service.
Rentals Strong at 63.46 — Up +7.05 from 56.41. Season average of 54.55 is +4.13 above last year's 50.42. One detailed comment praised the rental team for going above and beyond, including proactively calling a guest about skis left behind.
Lodge Comfort Reached 82.71 — The highest weekly F&B sub-score, up from 77.59 (+5.12). "Best lodges around!" "Most impressive bathroom we've ever been in." "Lodge is the best I've been to including those in Europe." The facilities continue to distinguish Snowbasin.
Service Held at 69.96 — Down only -1.29 from 71.25, maintaining the season's most consistent bright spot. Season average of 72.03 is +3.18 YoY. "In 51 years of skiing all over North America we have never experienced such a courteous and professional staff."
Retail Improved to 59.26 — Up +3.47 from 55.79 and above last year's comparable 58.43 (+0.83). Season average of 55.61 is nearly at parity with last year's 56.90 (-1.29).

Comment Analysis — 415 Total Comments

This week generated 142 dissatisfaction comments and 273 additional comments — the highest comment volume of the season. The season pass renewal theme dominates, but several recurring operational themes and new concerns emerged.

Themes of Praise

  1. Staff Excellence — The #1 theme for every week this season. "In 51 years of skiing all over North America we have never experienced such a courteous and professional staff." "Every employee was very helpful and friendly." "Snowbasin staff has done a fantastic job." First-time visitors consistently cite staff as the differentiator.
  2. Resilience in a Tough Year — "Snowbasin has done a REALLY GREAT job during a challenging snow year." "You deserve an award for covering the slopes and trails." "What you had to work with was phenomenal." Overwhelming good faith from guests about weather conditions.
  3. Facilities & Lodges — "Best lodges around." "The most impressive bathroom we've ever been in." "Lodge is the best I've been to including those in Europe." "Best Bathroom of Any Ski Area — that could be a great sticker!"
  4. Free Parking & Carpool — "Free parking is a huge reason I keep coming back." "Love the carpool lot!" "I've been skiing at Snowbasin for 50 years. Parking is still free and doesn't require a reservation. It's one of the main reasons I travel from Salt Lake."
  5. Safety Presence — "The safety patrollers in Bear Hollow create value on my season pass." "I appreciate the speed check on beginner trails." "Your efforts result in lower injuries on the mountain."
  6. Grooming & Snowmaking — "I was amazed how good the groomers were!" "You did an outstanding job on snowmaking — I am from the east where we thought we had perfected it." "Middle Bowl was epic!"
  7. Military/Veteran Pricing — Multiple appreciative mentions. "Thank you for the military season pass for veterans and their spouses!" "Please continue to provide discounts to active-duty military."
  8. RFID & Becker Upgrades — "Thanks for updating your ski pass scan system. It works better to have reader gates instead of individual scanners." Becker lift continues earning praise.

Themes of Concern

  1. Season Pass Renewal / Loyalty (NEW — TOP PRIORITY) — 8+ comments requesting renewal discounts, loyalty programs, or retention incentives. "After 12 years of constant season passes, I'm probably going to skip next year." "10-year holder looking elsewhere." Brighton cited for renewal discounts and higher young adult age threshold. Climate change concerns compound the renewal hesitancy. This is the #1 actionable theme of the week given the 26-27 sale is live.
  2. Snow Conditions / Limited Terrain — Persistent. Climate change explicitly mentioned by several guests. Elevation concerns ("80% of Snowbasin starts at 6,450 feet"). One former LCC skier says "I won't take that chance again." Not actionable weather-wise but directly feeds the renewal conversation above.
  3. Out-of-Control Skiers / Safety — "My husband was hit by a snowboarder who did not even stop." City Hill called an "I-15 deathtrap." Blue Grouse to Porky's Face cited as the worst intersection. Multiple requests for green coats specifically at Porky's. Ski teams singled out for excessive speed.
  4. F&B Pricing — "$11 for a Vitamin Water." "Fountain drinks over $5 and $20 for a burger." Several now bringing their own food. Season pass holder food discount (even 10%) suggested by multiple commenters. Not a change item, but the frequency remains high.
  5. Midweek Closures — "Closing lifts midweek to cater to the weekend crowd is not fair to season pass buyers." "Most of the mountain shut down during the week. It doesn't just seem to be snowpack; it seems to be saving on staff." This directly fuels pass holder frustration.
  6. Food Outages & Early Closures — Ran out of fries, shepherd's pie, hamburger buns. Food stations closed by 2 PM. Cinnabar closed early. Barista refused coffee at 3:01 PM. Pizza counter only prepping as items ran out. Strawberry food service remains inconsistent.
  7. Porcupine Lift — Helmet hitting roller stops, upgrade requests, speed concerns. Builds on last week's two-incident report. Becoming a recurring multi-week pattern.
  8. Wildcat Lot Issues — Porta-potties removed before season end. "PAVE THE WILDCAT PARKING LOT." Multiple mentions of potholes, mud, puddles. Also requested: restore bathroom access at lot.
  9. Green Coat Perception — "Your green coat crew is becoming unbearable" vs. "they create value on my season pass." Gap between waving vs. actual enforcement. Multiple requests to actually pull people over.
  10. Mountain Reporting Accuracy — "Website mountain report was never accurate." "Don't sugarcoat mountain conditions." Trail map needs redrawing. Signage on-mountain described as insufficient for wayfinding.

Recommended Action Items

STRATEGIC — Season Pass Renewal Messaging: With the 26-27 sale now live, this week's comments represent the most time-sensitive feedback of the season. Consider whether any retention-focused communication, appreciation gesture, or acknowledgment of the challenging year could be deployed to current pass holders. Even a personalized email from leadership acknowledging the difficult season would address the "feeling valued" gap multiple commenters describe.
IMMEDIATE — Unanswered Guest Emails: Follow up with the guest who tore their ACL and emailed hoping for a ticket discount with no reply. Separately, the Black Friday 2-day pass email took 10 days. Both are recoverable.
INVESTIGATE — F&B Regression Across All Outlets: Every outlet dropped this week — this isn't noise. John Paul Lodge fell 26 points. Diagnose whether it's staffing, supply chain, or weekend volume overwhelming capacity. Food outages (fries, shepherd's pie, buns) and early closures (food stations at 2 PM, Cinnabar early) suggest operational gaps that will be tested during spring break.
INVESTIGATE — Porcupine Lift Roller Stops: New safety concern: guest describes helmet repeatedly hitting roller stops. Combined with last week's two-incident report, this lift is generating multi-week safety feedback. Review roller stop positioning and loading procedures.
REVIEW — Little Wildcat Lift Closing Signage: A pass holder reconsidering two Premier renewals because of a 4:04 PM interaction. Installing a visible closing time sign at the lot entry or ski-away point is low-cost and prevents exactly this scenario.
REVIEW — "Phillip" in Ski Patrol: Two separate comments describe the same named individual as rude and argumentative. Worth a conversation with the ski patrol supervisor.
MONITOR — Wildcat Lot Amenities: Multiple complaints about porta-potties being removed before season end and paving requests. If any restroom solution can be reinstated for the remaining weeks, it would address a recurring complaint.

Looking Ahead

This was a defining week not because of the NPS numbers — which held essentially flat — but because of what the comments revealed. The 2026-27 season pass sale has catalyzed a wave of reflection from Snowbasin's most loyal customers. They love the resort. They love the staff. They praise the lodges as the best they've seen anywhere in the world. But after a punishing low-snow year, they want to feel recognized, and the absence of a renewal incentive or loyalty acknowledgment is a tangible gap in the competitive landscape.

The operational picture remains strong. The controllable metrics continue outperforming last season by meaningful margins — Parking (+9.80 YoY), F&B (+5.24), Safety (+3.93), Service (+3.18) — representing the best across-the-board execution in recent memory. The F&B pullback this week needs attention heading into spring break, but the season-level trend is solidly positive.

The response volume surge to 546 — 65% above the prior week — signals heightened engagement at exactly the moment renewal decisions are being made. Every guest interaction over the next few weeks carries disproportionate weight in those decisions. The Little Wildcat lift story is the perfect case study: one employee interaction at 4:04 PM on a Tuesday afternoon could cost two Premier pass sales.

Response Volume Note: This season has generated 3,629 responses through this date vs. 5,050 at the same point last year — a 28% reduction in survey volume reflecting lower visitation. The response mix has shifted notably this week: Snowbasin pass holders (Premier + Value) represented 53% of responses vs. 33% last week, likely driven by the pass sale launch.
