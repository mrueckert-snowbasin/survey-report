Week in Review

Our weekly NPS pulled back to 63.14 from last week's 68.54, a predictable reversion as the fresh snow that boosted last week's numbers gave way to deteriorating conditions. Snow Quality NPS crashed from +10.16 back to -37.12 (a 47-point swing), which dragged Mountain NPS down with it to 35.74 from 60.25. Despite this, the season average held steady at 63.74, and the year-over-year gap continued to narrow — now just 0.62 points behind last season's pace (64.36 at the comparable 3/5/25 dashboard).

The encouraging story this week: crowd-dependent metrics improved significantly. Lift Line NPS surged to 61.66 (+15.97), Trail Crowding improved to 15.00 (+11.82), and F&B seating satisfaction recovered to 26.97 (+12.22) — all reversing the heavy weekend-driven declines from two weeks ago. This suggests lower midweek-weighted volume this period. Parking also recovered to 61.63 (+2.98).

The biggest red flag: Likely to Return dropped sharply to 47.02 from 58.62 (-11.60). While some of this is seasonal fatigue and destination guests rating on geography, it warrants attention. Combined with snow quality struggles and end-of-season vibes, we should monitor whether this stabilizes or deteriorates further.

Season Context: Despite the second consecutive low-snow winter, our controllable metrics continue to dramatically outperform last season. Service (+4.22 YoY), Safety (+5.38), Parking (+11.53), and F&B (+6.53) are all tracking well ahead. The Mountain and Snow Quality gaps (-19.21 and -44.78 YoY respectively) are entirely weather-driven — last season had far more natural snow at this point.

Comment Analysis — 255 Total Comments

This week's comments (79 dissatisfaction responses + 176 additional comments) reveal clear themes. The dominant narrative remains the challenging snow year, but several actionable operational themes emerged.

Themes of Praise

  1. Staff Excellence — The single most consistent positive theme. "Your employee's attitudes and willingness to help was one of the strongest I've ever seen." Ticket staff rated "15 out of 10." Lifties, bussers, food workers, ski patrol — all praised across departments. Several first-time visitors say staff sets Snowbasin apart from other Utah resorts.
  2. Facilities & Lodge Quality — "World famous bathrooms," "country club level," "chandeliers are gorgeous." Real plates/flatware noted. Lodges consistently described as "next level beautiful" and the best at any resort guests have visited.
  3. Parking & Free Parking — Free parking remains a massive differentiator. "I couldn't believe how easy the parking was!" Military pricing also praised. Carpool system earning accolades even during higher-volume periods.
  4. Resilience Despite Snow — Many guests explicitly acknowledge the snow isn't the resort's fault and praise the team's effort. "Fantastic job for a challenging year." "You have been heroic with what you had to work with." Guests comparing to other Utah resorts say Snowbasin had more terrain open.
  5. Learn & Earn Program — Called a "SOLID WIN" by one detailed commenter. Praised for accessibility, affordability, and instructor quality. "The value is on point — it costs enough to make it not whimsical but still affordable enough for entry level."
  6. RFID Scanners & Becker Lift — "Thank you for finally installing RFID scanners!" and "Love the new RFID gates and Becker high speed lift" — guests noticing and appreciating infrastructure investments.
  7. Speed Monitors / Safety Presence — "I appreciate the speed check on beginner trails. As an instructor [at another resort] I wish our mountain provided that level of safety." "Very glad you have speed monitors on some slopes."

Themes of Concern

  1. Snow Conditions / Limited Terrain — Dominant negative theme. Icy conditions, exposed rocks/dirt, limited runs open, Strawberry Gondola closed. Not actionable (weather), but driving overall sentiment and the "Likely to Return" decline. Nearly every low NPS score references snow.
  2. Out-of-Control Skiers & Safety — Multiple detailed incidents. One guest hit by a snowboarder going ~40mph with no turns. Requests for more patrol presence at major intersections beyond Bear Hollow. Guests noting limited open terrain concentrates traffic and increases collision risk.
  3. Vegetarian/Vegan Options Lacking — This is an emerging, actionable trend. Six or more comments this week specifically cite lack of meat-free options. "No pizza, no soup, no salad, and the garden burger wasn't available." "Extremely limited vegetarian options." "More warm vegan options other than salad and fries." One guest did praise finding a vegan/GF pizza — suggesting options exist inconsistently.
  4. F&B Pricing — Persistent, not new. "$20 chili," "food is super expensive," "way too much food with the sandwich but paltry chili portion." Portion inconsistency may be more actionable than pricing itself.
  5. Food Outages & Slow Service — JP Lodge out of soup and hamburger buns. Cinnabar had an hour-long wait for food. Strawberry Philly sandwich stand lost an order for 25+ minutes. Pizza and soup stations at JP were unmanned.
  6. Green Jacket / Mountain Safety Perception — Polarized feedback. Some guests say "make it hard to enjoy the resort" and are "considering other resorts," while others praise the same program. Suggestion to acknowledge icy conditions make speed control harder, especially for snowboarders and kids.
  7. Porcupine Lift Condition — Safety concerns from two incidents in one trip (detailed above). Multiple unrelated commenters also request Porcupine replacement: "Porcupine next, please?" "Please prioritize replacing the Porcupine chair."
  8. Communication Gaps — Unanswered emails (2 instances). Lodge closure for private event not communicated to pass holders. Restroom closure for Needles wedding without adequate signage. Mountain/snow reporting described as "not helpful" — guests want actual conditions, not just "variable."
  9. Beginner Terrain Gap — Multiple comments about the jump from Little Cat to Wildcat being too big. Ea-sa-long and Penny Lane too narrow for beginners with faster traffic. "Dedicated beginner only runs" requested. City Hill congestion point not groomed.
  10. DJ Volume at Plaza — One very detailed, thoughtful comment requesting the DJ be relocated or volume significantly reduced. The plaza "should be a place for connection" and current volume "makes it difficult to have basic conversations."

Looking Ahead

We're entering March, historically when the season begins winding down. The snow quality reading of -37.12 and "Likely to Return" dropping to 47.02 suggest end-of-season fatigue is setting in, compounded by the challenging snow year. The playbook hasn't changed: keep doubling down on what we can control.

The season-to-date story remains remarkably strong given circumstances. The YoY gap of just 0.62 points (63.74 vs. 64.36) with dramatically less snow is a testament to the operational improvements across Service, Parking, Safety, and F&B. These are the metrics that will drive season pass renewals and word-of-mouth.

Key factors to watch in coming weeks: whether "Likely to Return" stabilizes, food supply chain consistency into the late season, and any further Porcupine lift incidents. If weather delivers any late-season snow, the mountain metrics should respond quickly — as last week's +47 point snow quality swing demonstrated.

Response Volume Note: This season has generated 3,061 responses through this date vs. 4,579 at the same point last year — a 33% reduction in survey volume. This likely reflects lower overall visitation due to snow conditions. The response mix is more balanced across pass types this year (Ikon 25%, Premier 23%, Lift Ticket 13%) vs. last year's heavier Ikon skew (40%).
